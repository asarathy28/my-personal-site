Soft Words

The gavel strikes, bringing all eyes to stare.
I rise slowly, my words soft but skin harsh.
But like dry heat that crawls across the bare
Desert sand, the courtroom is quick to parch
My voice and tell a tale of crimes I did
Not do. And their eyes, playing as God,
Gaze through their spectacles, ready to bid
A verdict, to tarnish a name, a fraud.
But I, seemingly passive and resigned,
Rage under my mask, seeking for justice.
King of my fate, king of my name, Bull Shit,
I see my brothers waiting in my shroud.

Do not let truth be lost to the Black Sea.

Listen to my soft words, let us be free.
